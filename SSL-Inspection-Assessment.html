How to Read Your SSL Audit Report

Your report shows inspected (HTTPS) vs uninspected (SSL) traffic side by side. Here's what to focus on.

1

Protocol Breakdown

Compare the SSL bar (uninspected) to HTTPS (inspected). The ratio tells you your inspection coverage. If SSL is larger than HTTPS ‚Äî most of your traffic is flying blind. Ideal state: HTTPS dominates with SSL being a thin sliver of known exemptions.

2

Uninspected Cloud Applications

This widget shows which SaaS apps are bypassing inspection. Expected bypasses: M365 apps with certificate pinning (handled by click-to-run rules). Red flags: seeing major SaaS apps here that should be inspected ‚Äî Salesforce, ServiceNow, Box, Slack, etc.

3

Threats Blocked vs Threats in Uninspected Traffic

This is the key comparison. The left shows threats Zscaler caught because you're inspecting. The right shows threat signals in traffic you're NOT inspecting ‚Äî these represent active risk in your environment.

4

URL Categories ‚Äî Inspected vs Uninspected

The pie charts reveal which categories are bypassed. Look for large uninspected categories that aren't regulatory requirements. Common culprits: Miscellaneous, Web Search, Streaming Media, Content Delivery Networks. These are often bypassed "just in case" and never revisited.

5

File Types

If you see file downloads (EXE, DLL, ZIP, Office docs) in uninspected traffic ‚Äî that's active risk. Malware hides in these file types and Sandbox can't analyze what it can't see.

URL Category Inspection Guide

Not all bypasses are equal. Here's how to prioritize which categories to inspect first.

Category Recommendation Why
Miscellaneous / Other Inspect Now Uncategorized = unknown risk. This is where novel threats hide. No regulatory reason to bypass.
Web Search Inspect Now Search engines are a common malware delivery vector via malvertising. High volume, high value.
Content Delivery Networks Inspect Now CDNs host legitimate and malicious content. Attackers use CDN domains to look trustworthy.
Streaming Media Legitimate Bypass Low security value ‚Äî streaming content is not a significant threat vector. Often doesn't respond well to inspection. Bypass is expected and acceptable.
Newly Registered Domains Inspect Now Top phishing vector. These should be inspected AND isolated (with CBI) or cautioned at minimum.
SaaS Apps (general) Inspect Now DLP can't protect data in SaaS apps without inspection. Critical for data security posture.
Microsoft 365 Legitimate Bypass Certificate pinning requires bypass. Enable M365 click-to-run rules. Use out-of-band CASB for visibility.
Conferencing (Teams/Zoom) Legitimate Bypass Real-time media doesn't benefit from inline inspection. Bypass is expected and acceptable.
Health / Medical Regulatory Bypass HIPAA/PII concerns may require bypass. Document the regulatory justification. Consider out-of-band scanning.
Finance / Banking Regulatory Bypass PCI/regulatory requirements may mandate bypass. Document justification. Review annually.
Custom bypass lists Review Needed These accumulate over time and rarely get cleaned up. Audit each entry ‚Äî is the original reason still valid?
All other categories Inspect Now If there's no regulatory or technical reason to bypass, inspect it. Zscaler recommends 100% inspection as the goal.
üîë The Golden Rule

Every bypass should have a documented reason. If you can't explain why a category is bypassed, it should be inspected. Legacy bypasses from initial deployment are the #1 source of unnecessary blind spots.

4-Week SSL Inspection Expansion Plan

A phased approach to closing inspection gaps safely ‚Äî no big-bang changes.

üí° Tip: Use Ctrl+P to print this plan as a checklist for your weekly review meetings
üîê Prerequisite: CA Certificate Deployment

SSL inspection requires the Zscaler root or intermediate CA certificate to be trusted by all endpoints and browsers. Without it, users will see certificate warnings on every inspected site. This is the #1 operational blocker for SSL inspection expansion.

What To Deploy
Download your tenant's Zscaler root CA certificate from Administration ‚Üí SSL Inspection ‚Üí Intermediate Root Certificate Authority. Deploy via GPO (Windows), MDM profile (macOS/mobile), or certificate management platform.
How To Verify
Visit ip.zscaler.com from an inspected endpoint. The certificate chain should show your Zscaler CA ‚Äî not the origin site's CA. If you see the origin CA, that traffic is not being inspected.
Week 1
Baseline & Quick Wins
  • Import the SSL Audit Report and review current inspection ratio
  • Check your grade: Analytics ‚Üí Security Policy Audit Report ‚Üí Traffic Inspection
  • Enable M365 click-to-run if not already configured (Policy ‚Üí URL & Cloud App Control ‚Üí Advanced)
  • Verify "Zscaler Recommended Exemption Rule" is enabled (handles cert-pinning automatically)
  • Inspect: Miscellaneous, Web Search, and Content Delivery Networks if currently bypassed
üí° Why This Is Safe

"These categories have no regulatory bypass requirement. Miscellaneous traffic is uncategorized ‚Äî by definition, it's unknown risk. Web Search and CDNs are high-volume vectors for malvertising and malware delivery. Enabling inspection here gives Zscaler visibility into your biggest blind spots with minimal user impact."

Week 2
Expand Coverage
  • Enable inspection for Newly Registered Domains if currently bypassed
  • Review SaaS applications in the uninspected cloud apps widget ‚Äî enable inspection for non-pinned apps
  • Monitor web logs for SSL Policy Reason field ‚Äî identify any certificate errors or user complaints
  • Create specific bypass rules for any legitimate breakage (app-specific, not category-wide)
üí° Why This Works

"If a specific app breaks, we create a narrow bypass just for that app ‚Äî we don't bypass the entire category. The goal is surgical exemptions, not blanket bypasses."

Week 3
Audit Legacy Bypasses
  • Review all custom SSL bypass rules ‚Äî when were they created? By whom? Is the reason still valid?
  • Review certificate pinning exemptions ‚Äî the industry is moving away from pinning, some old exemptions may no longer be needed
  • Check source-based exemptions ‚Äî are entire locations or groups bypassed that shouldn't be?
  • Document every remaining bypass with a business justification
üí° Why This Works

"Bypass rules are like firewall rules ‚Äî they accumulate and nobody cleans them up. A rule that made sense 2 years ago during initial deployment may be completely unnecessary now. Let's review them together."

Week 4
Measure & Report
  • Pull the SSL Audit Report again ‚Äî compare inspected vs uninspected ratio to Week 1 baseline
  • Check Security Policy Audit Report grade ‚Äî did it improve?
  • Quantify: "X additional threats blocked that we would have missed before"
  • Document remaining legitimate bypasses with justification for compliance records
  • Set calendar reminder: re-audit in 90 days
üí° The Executive Summary

"In 4 weeks we went from X% to Y% inspection coverage. We identified Z additional threats in traffic that was previously uninspected. Every remaining bypass is documented with a business justification. We'll re-audit quarterly."
